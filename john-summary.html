Summary

Keyword

God

Purpose

To present Jesus as the incarnate lord. Clearly evangelistic in purpose (20:31), the Gospel was written to bring its readers to faith and life in Jesus as the Christ, the Son of God, the revelation of grace and truth.

Author/Date

John | Written sometime between AD 80-90

Outline

  1. The Public Ministry of Jesus (1-12)
  1. The Private Ministry of Jesus - Upper Room (13-16)
  1. The Priestly Prayer of Jesus (17)
  1. The Passion and Resurrection of Jesus (18-21)

Key Passages

1:1

“In the beginning was the Word”—the pre-existence and equality of the Word with God.

1:1-18

The Logos prologue.

3:3

The necessity of sovereign regeneration: “You must be born again!”

3:16

God’s love sent Christ to the cross

8:58

“Before Abraham was born, I am’”

10:11

“I am the good shepherd” who “lays down His life for the sheep”

10:30

“I and the Father are one”—Christ asserts His deity.

14:6

Jesus is the Way, Truth, and Life.

17:3

This is eternal life: to know God and Jesus Christ.

20:30-31

The purpose of Gospel: eternal life in Christ.

21:15-19

“Peter, do you love Me?” Peter’s restoration.

Key People

Lazarus

Brother of Mary and Martha; died and was raised again by Jesus in John 11.

Mary Magdalene

First to see risen Christ (had 7 demons cast from her in Luke 8).

Nicodemus

Teacher of the Law who asked how can we be born again?
